Chest shops are the backbone of the Praeto economy. A chest shop is a player-owned physical shop in the world: a chest paired with a sign that lets other players buy from or sell to the shop owner at a fixed diamond price. Trades happen instantly — no auctions, no negotiation.

## Shop Types

Every chest shop has one of two types:

| Type | What it means |
|---|---|
| **SELLING** | The shop owner is selling items to other players. You pay diamonds and receive items. |
| **BUYING** | The shop owner is buying items from other players. You hand over items and receive diamonds. |

<Info>
  A single shop can only be one type. Many traders set up pairs of shops — one SELLING and one BUYING — to create a buy/sell spread on the same item.
</Info>

## Shop Data Fields

Each shop exposes the following data, both in-game and through the API:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `shop_id` | Unique identifier for the shop |
| `owner_name` | Display name of the shop owner |
| `owner_uuid` | UUID of the shop owner |
| `world` | The world the shop is located in |
| `x`, `y`, `z` | Coordinates of the shop chest |
| `shop_type` | `SELLING` or `BUYING` |
| `price` | Cost in diamonds per transaction |
| `remaining` | Current stock available (for SELLING shops) |
| `item_type` | The Minecraft item type identifier |
| `item_name` | Human-readable item name |
| `item_quantity` | Number of items per transaction |
| `snbt` | Raw NBT data describing the item |
| `last_seen` | Timestamp of the last time the shop was detected active |

## Finding Shops

Use the in-game shop browser to search for shops by item name or type. The browser shows each shop's location, price per unit, stock remaining, and owner.

<Tip>
  Always check the `remaining` stock before making a long trip to a SELLING shop. A shop with zero stock can't complete your trade, and you'll have wasted the journey.
</Tip>

You can also query shops programmatically using the transaction lookup API:

```http
GET /v1/transactions/lookup/shop?item=DARK_OAK_LOG&shop_type=buyFromShop
```

This returns a list of matching shops sorted by price, letting you compare options before you head out.

## Creating a Shop

<Steps>
  <Step title="Place a chest">
    Put a chest in an accessible location in your base or shop building. Make sure players can reach it without trespassing into protected areas.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stock the chest">
    Fill the chest with the items you want to sell (for a SELLING shop), or leave it with diamonds to fund purchases (for a BUYING shop).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach a shop sign">
    Place a sign on the chest face. Use the correct sign format for the Praeto shop plugin — include your price in diamonds and the item quantity per transaction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the listing">
    Right-click the sign to confirm the shop registered correctly. The sign will display the item name, price, and current stock once active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What the Shop Sign Displays

When a shop is active, its sign shows:

```
[Shop Owner Name]
[Item Quantity] x [Item Name]
[Price] diamonds
[Stock Remaining / "OUT OF STOCK"]
```

## Managing Your Shop

<Accordion title="Restocking a SELLING shop">
  Open the chest and add more items directly. The stock count updates automatically — there is no need to recreate the sign.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Changing the price">
  Remove the existing sign and place a new one with the updated price. The shop will re-register with the new price on the next detection cycle.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Closing a shop">
  Break the sign to deactivate the shop. Remove your remaining stock from the chest before breaking the sign to avoid item loss.
</Accordion>

<Warning>
  If your SELLING shop runs out of stock, it becomes unavailable to buyers but stays visible in search results. Restock promptly to avoid losing customers to competing shops.
</Warning>
